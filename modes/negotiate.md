# negotiate.md — Salary Negotiation for Indian Market
> Trigger: "I got an offer", "they gave me X LPA", "how do I negotiate", /negotiate

## Indian Negotiation Context
- HR anchors low expecting negotiation. First number is rarely final.
- Asking 15–25% above offer is normal and expected.
- "Rigid band" is usually a tactic, not a fact.
- What you can negotiate beyond base salary:
  - Joining bonus (to offset unvested ESOPs or notice period)
  - Variable pay structure (fixed vs variable split)
  - ESOP grant size + cliff
  - WFH flexibility (real monetary value — factor rent/commute)
  - Role title (affects future job search)
  - 6-month review instead of 12

## Step 1 — Gather Offer Details
Ask:
1. Exact offer (CTC breakdown: base, variable, ESOP, joining bonus)?
2. Company tier (funding stage, size)?
3. Competing offers or currently employed?
4. Current CTC?
5. Minimum acceptable number?
6. Target number?
7. Decision deadline?

## Step 2 — Analyze the Offer
```
Offered CTC: {X LPA} | Your target: {Y LPA} | Gap: {Z LPA}
In-hand estimate: {0.65–0.72 × X} LPA
Market benchmark: {X–Y LPA for this role+tier+city}
Assessment: Fair / Below market / Above market

Your leverage:
- Competing offer: yes/no + how much
- Currently employed: yes/no
- Notice period urgency for them
- Rare skills you have
```

## Step 3 — Negotiation Scripts

### Script A — Standard counter (10–20% below target)
"Thank you for the offer — I'm excited about the role. After reviewing, I was hoping we could get closer to [X LPA]. This reflects market rates for this role at [company stage] and what I bring in [specific skill]. Is there flexibility to move toward [X]?"

### Script B — Competing offer in hand
"I want to be transparent — I have another offer at [Y LPA]. [Company] is my first choice because [specific reason]. If we can get to [X LPA], I can commit immediately."

### Script C — Salary band is genuinely fixed
"I understand the base is fixed at [X]. Could we explore: a joining bonus of [amount]? A 6-month instead of 12-month review? An increase in ESOP grant from [current] to [target]?"

## Step 4 — Response Handling
- "Band is fixed": Ask specifically about other components. Move to Script C.
- Push back on counter: come down 30–40% of the gap, not all the way.
- Hard deadline: "Can I have until [specific date]?" — almost always works.

## Step 5 — Decision Framework
- Final offer ≥ your minimum: Accept.
- 5–10% below minimum: Evaluate non-cash value (growth, brand, learning, WFH).
- >10% below minimum: Walk away. Underpaying now anchors every future salary.
Long-term cost: every 1 LPA left on table today ≈ 3–5 LPA lost over next 3 job changes.
